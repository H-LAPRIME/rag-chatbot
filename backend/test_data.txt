ACADEMIC PROGRAMS
-----------------

The Computer Science Department offers a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program. This is a 4-year degree program that covers programming, algorithms, data structures, software engineering, and computer systems. Graduates can pursue careers as software developers, systems analysts, database administrators, and IT consultants.

The Mathematics Department offers a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics program. This is a 2-year degree program focusing on mathematical modeling, numerical analysis, and computational methods. Career outcomes include roles as data scientists, quantitative analysts, research mathematicians, and operations researchers.

The Physics Department offers a Bachelor of Science in Physics program. This is a 4-year degree program covering classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism. Graduates can work as research scientists, physics teachers, laboratory technicians, or continue to graduate studies.

The Engineering Department offers a Bachelor of Engineering in Civil Engineering program. This is a 4-year degree program that includes structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, transportation engineering, and environmental engineering. Career outcomes include civil engineer, structural engineer, project manager, and construction manager.

COURSES INFORMATION
-------------------

In the Computer Science program, students must take CS101 - Introduction to Programming. This is a 3-credit course offered in Fall semester. The course covers basic programming concepts, data types, control structures, and functions. It is a foundational course for all computer science students.

CS201 - Data Structures and Algorithms is a 4-credit course offered in Spring semester. This course covers arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, and various algorithms. Prerequisites include CS101.

CS301 - Database Systems is a 3-credit course offered in Fall semester. Students learn about relational databases, SQL, database design, normalization, and transaction management. This course requires CS201 as a prerequisite.

MATH201 - Linear Algebra is a 3-credit course offered in Fall and Spring semesters. This course covers vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and linear transformations. It is part of the Mathematics program.

MATH301 - Differential Equations is a 3-credit course offered in Spring semester. Topics include first-order differential equations, second-order linear equations, systems of differential equations, and applications. Prerequisites: MATH201.

PHYS101 - General Physics I is a 4-credit course offered in Fall semester. This course covers mechanics, kinematics, dynamics, work and energy, and rotational motion. It includes both lecture and laboratory components.

PHYS201 - Electricity and Magnetism is a 4-credit course offered in Spring semester. Topics include electric fields, magnetic fields, electromagnetic induction, and Maxwell's equations. Prerequisites: PHYS101.

ENG101 - Introduction to Engineering is a 2-credit course offered in Fall semester. This course introduces students to engineering principles, design process, and engineering ethics.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
---------------------------

Q: What are the office hours for the Computer Science department?
A: The department office is open Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Individual faculty members have specific office hours posted on their office doors and course websites.

Q: How do I register for courses?
A: Course registration is done online through the student portal. Registration opens approximately one month before the start of each semester. You will receive an email notification when registration opens.

Q: What is the deadline for dropping a course?
A: You can drop a course without penalty within the first two weeks of the semester. After that, you may withdraw with a W grade until the midterm exam period.

Q: Are there tutoring services available?
A: Yes, the university offers free tutoring services for mathematics, physics, and computer science courses. Tutoring sessions are available in the Learning Center, Building E, Room 100, Monday through Friday from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Q: How can I apply for scholarships?
A: Scholarship applications are available through the financial aid office. Most scholarships require maintaining a minimum GPA and submitting an application form along with recommendation letters. Deadlines vary by scholarship type.
